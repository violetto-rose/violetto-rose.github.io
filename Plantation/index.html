About Our Project

Welcome to our plantation project by 2nd year Computer Science and Design students (Group 5). We've adopted and are nurturing a Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum wallisii) as part of our commitment to environmental sustainability.

Our project aims to promote awareness about the importance of plants in our ecosystem and encourage more people to adopt and care for plants in their daily lives.

Peace Lily: Nature's Air Purifier

Origin of the Plant

Spathiphyllum wallisii, commonly known as peace lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas, primarily found in Central and South America. It was first described by the German-Belgian botanist Gustav Wallis in the 19th century and was named after him.

Native to: Central and South America

Usage in Daily Life

Peace lilies are popular indoor plants due to their attractive foliage and white flowers. They are commonly used as decorative elements in homes, offices, and public spaces because of their ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Besides their aesthetic appeal, peace lilies are also known for their air-purifying qualities, helping to filter toxins from indoor air, making them a practical and aesthetically pleasing addition to indoor environments.

Removes toxins like benzene, formaldehyde, and trichloroethylene

Appearance in Literature and Folklore

Peace lilies don't have a significant presence in traditional folklore or ancient literature, as they are a relatively modern discovery and cultivated plant species.

In contemporary literature and cultural references, peace lilies often symbolize tranquility, peace, and purity due to their elegant white flowers and graceful appearance. Their association with peace has led to their inclusion in various modern works of fiction, poems, and sometimes as symbolic elements in art or storytelling to represent harmony or serenity.

Symbolizes: Peace, purity, and prosperity

Care Guide

Watering

Water when the top inch of soil is dry. Peace lilies will droop when thirsty.

Light

Thrives in low to medium indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight.

Temperature

Prefers 65-85°F (18-29°C). Keep away from cold drafts.

Fertilizer

Feed monthly during growing season with diluted houseplant fertilizer.
